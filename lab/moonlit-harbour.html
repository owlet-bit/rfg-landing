Understanding Color Theory

The science and art behind personal color analysis.

The 12-Season System

Personal color analysis divides human coloring into 12 "seasons" based on three dimensions:

1. Temperature (Warm vs. Cool)

The most fundamental division. Warm coloring has golden, yellow, or peachy undertones. Cool coloring has blue, pink, or rosy undertones. You are definitively cool.

2. Value (Light vs. Deep)

How light or dark your overall coloring is. You sit in the medium range‚Äîdark hair but fair skin creates balanced depth.

3. Chroma (Muted vs. Clear)

How saturated or grayed-out your coloring appears. You're on the clearer side‚Äîyour features have definition, not haziness.

Why True Summer, Not Soft Summer?

This is a common question because both are cool seasons. Here's the distinction:

Your hair has definition (not soft), your eye colors are distinct (not blended), and your skin-to-hair contrast is noticeable. Soft Summer colors would make you look flat.

Why Not Cool Winter?

Cool Winter requires high contrast (think Snow White: black hair, white skin). Your contrast is medium-high, not extreme. Winter colors would be too stark and harsh.

The Science

This isn't magic‚Äîit's physics. Colors reflect onto your skin. Cool colors (blue-based) reflect blue light onto your cool skin, harmonizing. Warm colors (yellow-based) reflect yellow light, which clashes and creates a "muddy" effect.
