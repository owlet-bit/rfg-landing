Why these colors work (science, not vibes)

Built from undertone • chroma • value • eye-echo

1) Undertone harmony: your skin reads cool-neutral. Strong warm orange/yellow pigments can look “sallow” or “ruddy,” while cool-neutral pigments read clean.
2) Chroma match: your features tolerate more clarity than Soft Summer (you don’t need corpse-grey dust). So we keep saturation moderate—alive, but not neon.
3) Value range: you sit in a medium value band. Optic white/ink black create harsh edges; soft navy + charcoal give drama without stealing your face.
4) Eye echo: your iris contains olive-gold, sage/khaki, and slate. Best colors repeat those wavelengths: blue-greens, blue-greys, cool reds, clear roses.

Makeup Map

Cool Summer rules

Your face loves cool rose, rose-coral (pink-based), taupe, mushroom, pewter, slate, cool berry red. Avoid strongly warm bronzes/oranges unless heavily toned down (think “antique,” not “sunset”).

Style Elements (based on your features)

Structure + clarity, softened edges

You read refined + slightly sharp with a cool palette. Best style is structure without harshness.

  • Necklines: open and clean — scoop, soft V, wide boat; avoid tiny busy necklines.
  • Buttons/Hardware: brushed, matte, or softly polished; minimal yellow shine.
  • Jewelry shapes: elongated + sculptural (teardrops, sleek ovals) over tiny sparkly clusters.
  • Prints: watercolor, blurred florals, heathered plaids; avoid high-contrast comic-book graphics.
  • Textures: washed silk, cashmere, brushed wool, matte leather; avoid plasticky shine.
  • Frames: smoky crystal, pewter metal, cool soft tortoise.

Metals

Cool shine, smoked not yellow

Best: brushed silver, pewter, white gold, platinum. If gold: choose antique/champagne (muted) not bright yellow.
