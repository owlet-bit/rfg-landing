Body Colors (your real pigments)

These anchor the whole palette

Veins: inner wrist can read more violet (thin skin + light); forearm often reads blue-green/teal. Both can be true at once. Your overall signal is cool-neutral with a blue-green bias.

Why your best colors work (science, not “mauve prison”)

undertone • chroma • value • eye-echo

1) Undertone harmony: Your base reads cool-neutral. Too-warm pigments (orange/yellow) create surface muddiness.
2) Mineral chroma: You asked for slightly more saturation than classic Soft Summer. Correct. You handle clear, cool pigment (teal, mineral blue, rose-coral) as long as it’s not neon.
3) Value control: Your drama lives in ink navy / deep teal / graphite, not black.
4) Eye echo: Your iris carries sage + slate + blue-green; the palette repeats those wavelengths so your face looks “assembled,” not fought with.
5) The pink issue: Your “coral” is rose-coral (pink-based) — not orange-coral. Mauves can exist, but they are not your whole personality.

If a “summer” palette looks like a Victorian ghost, it’s not you. This one is built to be alive.

Makeup Map

Cool Summer with rose-coral reds

Best effect on you is clean pigment + controlled softness: rose-coral blush, cool berry reds, taupe/pewter eyes, slate liner, blue-green accents.

Style Elements (based on your features)

Structure + sleekness + controlled drama

Your coloring reads refined + cinematic when styling is clean, intentional, and not fussy. Think: sharp lines, smooth fabrics, sculptural details — but in your cool palette.

  • Necklines: open + clean (soft V, wide scoop, boat, square). Avoid tiny cluttered necklines.
  • Silhouettes: defined waist, long vertical lines, wrap/structured drape. (Soft-Dramatic friendly.)
  • Hardware: brushed/satin finishes; avoid bright yellow gold shine.
  • Jewelry: elongated sculptural shapes (ovals, drops, curved daggers), not tiny glitter clusters.
  • Prints: high-end blur: watercolor florals, marbling, heathered checks. Avoid high-contrast comic prints.
  • Best “drama” move: deep teal or ink navy column + rose-coral mouth.

Metals

Smoked shine beats yellow shine

Best: platinum, white gold, brushed silver, pewter. Gold can work only as soft champagne (muted), never bright yellow.
