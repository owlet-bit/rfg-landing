Your Coloring, Corrected

What two professional drapings and a decade of training actually tell us

Undertone: Cool-neutral, not warm-neutral. You have a warm overtone — peachiness, golden quality — sitting on top of a cool undertone. This is why warm colors don't clash but cool-neutral colors make you luminous. The spring modifier means a touch of yellow warmth in your cool base, which is why you can wear corals and warm browns that a pure Light Summer couldn't.

Dominant characteristic: Lightness. Confirmed by every draping exercise. Depth overwhelms you unless it's in cool or neutral hues with enough contrast to justify it.

Contrast capacity: Higher than standard Light Summer. Your dark brows against fair skin and the intensity of your green eyes mean you can handle depth for drama — midnight navy, forest velvet, deep wine, true black — that would bury a non-contrasting Light Summer.

Chroma: Mixed. You need both muted mid-tones for everyday AND clear, saturated accents for impact. This is why neither Light Summer (too soft) nor Bright Winter (too intense) felt complete — you require both registers.

Your makeup should be muted and cool-toned in its foundations, with the capacity to go deep and dramatic through contrast. Soft daily, striking when you want to be.

Base

Cool-neutral undertone, luminous finish
Foundation Satin or luminous finish, light-medium coverage. Your undertone is cool-neutral — avoid strongly yellow or strongly pink bases. Look for "neutral" or "cool neutral" shade matches. Your skin's natural luminosity is an asset; don't bury it.
Concealer Brightening under-eye concealer with a slightly peachy corrector underneath — the peach counteracts the blue-purple of under-eye darkness on cool skin. Spot conceal redness on the forehead and nose rather than full coverage everywhere.
Powder Minimal. T-zone only. Finely milled translucent — not yellow-toned. Never powder cheekbones or under-eyes.
Skip: heavily warm/yellow foundations, full matte coverage, thick powder

Your Looks — The Velvet Siren

Seven looks from everyday to stage, all recalibrated

The Everyday

Luminous skin-tint, cream dusty rose blush high on cheeks. Champagne wash on lids, mushroom in crease. Brown-black mascara, curled. Cool taupe brow gel. Mauve rose balm. You look polished, soft, and effortlessly right.

The Polished Professional

Rose taupe blended through the crease, pebble in the outer corner, champagne on the lid. Mushroom liner smudged along upper lashes. Dusty rose blush. Soft coral satin lip. This is the warm register — the spring modifier doing its quiet work.

The Coral Moment

Champagne wash on lids, mascara only. Pink champagne blush. Soft coral or dusty coral lip at full saturation. The lip leads. The face supports. This is what worked last night.

The Velvet Siren

Coffee or dark chocolate smoked through the crease and outer corner. Rose taupe on lid. Pebble liner smudged. Mauve rose blush. Deep wine or cranberry lip, satin finish. Your green eyes against the cool smoke with that dark lip — this is romantic severity in action.

The Forest Witch

Forest velvet or dark sage smudged along upper lashes as liner. Mushroom on lid. Mauve rose blush. Deep wine lip. The green liner activates your irises through adjacent-hue intensification. Witchy, grounded, and entirely yours.

The Indigo Dream

Deep indigo smoked on outer lid and crease. Orchid or soft lavender on inner lid. Dusty rose blush. Cranberry lip. Your drama register at full power — the contrast between the light lavender and deep indigo uses your contrast capacity and the purple family flatters your cool undertone.

Stage / Comedy

Build from the Professional or Velvet Siren with more intensity. Deeper crease, stronger liner, extra coat of black mascara. Warm pink or soft coral blush (reads better under stage lights than dusty rose, which can wash out). True red lip — blue-based, satin. Keep the face warm and alive from the back row without going warm-toned in the base.

The Governing Principles — Revised

Updated for cool-neutral undertone with spring modifier

Cool-neutral base, warm accents. Your default should be cool-neutral (mushroom, rose taupe, dusty rose, mauve). Your spring modifier allows warm accents (soft coral, champagne, bronze eye shadow) but they're the exception, not the foundation.

Muted for everyday, saturated for impact. Your daily palette is dusty and soft. Your drama palette is clear and deep. You need both registers, and the contrast between them is what makes you a Velvet Siren rather than a standard Light Summer.

Lift, always lift. Same as before. Every placement pulls upward and outward on your long oval face.

One star, one supporting cast. Still applies. Bold eye or bold lip, not both.

Both metals work. Your neutral undertone means silver AND gold flatter you. Silver is technically more harmonious with your cool base; gold works because of the spring modifier. Wear both. Mix them.

Your depth capacity is real. Standard Light Summer advice would tell you to avoid dark colors. You can wear midnight navy, deep wine, forest velvet, true black, and espresso because you have the contrast for it. This is what makes you contrasting Light Summer, not just Light Summer.
