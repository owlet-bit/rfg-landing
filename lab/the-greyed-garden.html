Reading Your Colors

What I see when I look at you across twenty different photos, lighting conditions, with and without makeup.

You're not a Summer. You're a greyed garden.

The conventional wisdom would slot you into Soft Summer and call it done. But that misses what's actually happening. Your coloring isn't soft because it lacks intensity — it's soft because it has complexity. There's no single dominant note. Everything is layered: cool over warm, muted over clear, green shifting to gold shifting back to gray.

Your eyes are the tell. They're not simply "hazel" — they're a whole weather system. The outer ring reads as a greyed sage-olive, the middle band shifts between warm ochre and cool moss depending on light, and there's genuine amber fire in the inner starburst. When you wear green, the green in your eyes wakes up. When you wear rose, the warmth comes forward. They're reactive, not static.

Your skin reads cool-neutral with visible pink in the cheeks and chest — the kind that flushes easily and shows every emotion. But it's not purely cool; there's a warmth underneath, like a peach behind gauze. This is why both silver AND champagne gold work on you, though silver has the edge.

The key insight: you need colors with gray in them. Not gray as in dull — gray as in complex. A greyed teal, a greyed raspberry, a greyed sage. Colors that have been weathered, softened, lived-in. Nothing straight off the color wheel. Everything filtered through morning fog.

  • SeasonSoft Summer Deep (neutral-cool)
  • UndertoneCool-neutral with warm undercurrent
  • ContrastLow-medium (avoid stark extremes)
  • SaturationMuted — always grayed, never pure
  • Value RangeMedium — both light and dark work, extremes don't
  • Best MetalsOxidized silver, pewter, champagne
