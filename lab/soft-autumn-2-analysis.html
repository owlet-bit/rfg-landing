Warm Soft / Muted

Temperature: Warm throughout. Skin (12°), hair (15°), and eyes (13°) all sit in the same narrow warm hue band. The green iris component is the only cool element, and it's embedded within warm-toned eyes.

Contrast: Skin-to-hair value gap is 40 points (72% vs 32%) — this is moderate contrast. Not high-drama, not blended-soft. You have enough contrast for definition without starkness.

Chroma: Low-medium across the board (28–39%). This is a muted, soft palette — think watercolor rather than oil paint. Colors are warm but gentle, never loud.

Dimensional range: Hair shows the widest value spread (16%–51%), giving natural dimension and movement. Skin is more consistent (63%–84%). Eyes are the darkest feature.

In color analysis terms: Warm undertone, soft/muted chroma, moderate contrast, moderate value depth. The hazel eyes with their green-gold starburst are the wild card — they introduce a cool complexity into an otherwise warm-dominant palette.
