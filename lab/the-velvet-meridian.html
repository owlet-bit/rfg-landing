Understanding Your Chromatic Identity

Lauren, your coloring exists in a realm that most seasonal systems struggle to capture. You are not simply warm or cool—you are atmospherically complex, like the light at the edge of a forest clearing just before sunset.

Your skin possesses a remarkable quality: translucent depth with chameleon undertones. The visible blue-green veining at your wrists speaks to cool foundations, yet your skin drinks in golden light like parched earth welcomes rain. This duality is rare and profoundly beautiful.

Your eyes are your true compass—grey-green hazel with amber starbursts that shift between steel, sage, and gold depending on your palette. They reveal the truth: you belong to the Soft Summer family, but with a depth and warmth that pulls you toward its Deep expression.

I've named your palette The Velvet Meridian—that liminal line where the softness of twilight meets the grounded richness of moss and earth. Your colors have weight but never heaviness, complexity but never confusion.

Dominant Dimension
Soft

Your coloring demands colors that have been touched by grey—not dull, but sophisticated. High chroma shocks your system; muted tones make you luminous.

Secondary Dimension
Cool-Neutral

You lean cool, but with a flexibility that allows carefully selected neutral-warm tones. Think teal over turquoise, dusty rose over bubblegum pink.

Tertiary Dimension
Medium-Deep

Your contrast is moderate, allowing you to wear both lighter and darker values—but your true power lives in the mid-to-deep range where colors develop richness.

Contrast Level
Moderate-High

Your ash-brown hair against fair skin with those multitonal eyes creates meaningful contrast. Monochromatic looks flatten you; strategic contrast ignites you.
