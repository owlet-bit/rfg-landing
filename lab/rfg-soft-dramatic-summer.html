The Column & The Spell Soft Dramatic dressing law

Two layers, two jobs: Column = structure (the base color architecture). Spell = activation (the precise accent). They are linked, but not the same thing.

1) The Column structure

The column is your base architecture: one dominant color family creating a long vertical line. It does the heavy lifting—length, authority, inevitability.

  • One dominant color family only
  • Head-to-toe or nearly head-to-toe
  • Minimal contrast breaks
  • Shoes match or deepen the column
  • Texture can vary; color should not

2) Your Best Column Colors carry the outfit

These can hold an entire look on you without flattening your softness.

  • Indigo Ink
  • Deep Navy / Storm Navy
  • Rosewood Smoke
  • Smoked Plum
  • Graphite / Charcoal Blue
  • Deep Petrol Teal

3) The Spell activation

The spell is the intentional accent that makes the column memorable. Without it, you can read severe. With too many, the system breaks.

  • One spell per outfit
  • Two max only if one is makeup
  • Spell is lighter/juicier/more luminous than the column
  • Spell must not destroy the vertical line

4) Your Spell Families signal

Glow: Moonlit Oat, Chamomile Silk, Pearl Ash
Juicy: Cool Rosy Coral, Raspberry Smoke, Rosewater Mauve
Edge: Petrol Teal (with navy/graphite/plum), Indigo Ink (with rosewood/plum)

Law: Column = containment Spell = signal

Column + Spell examples (fast)
  • Indigo Ink column + Moonlit Oat cami → authority softened by light
  • Deep Navy column + Cool Rosy Coral lip → power + vitality
  • Rosewood Smoke column + Pearl Ash earrings → sensual + cerebral
  • Graphite column + Petrol Teal liner → severe + electric

RFG Systems (Custom)

This is the part that’s not generic color analysis. It’s the operating system: how you deploy color for authority, magnetism, and mythic presence.

Lauren’s “Open-Loop” Colors memory residue

These keep people from finishing the mental file on you. They remember you, but can’t summarize you — which makes them replay you.

  • Indigo Ink
  • Petrol Teal
  • Deep Mauve Smoke
  • Rosewood Smoke
  • Storm Lavender-Blue

RFG Heat Dial when to deploy

Low heat (authority): navy/indigo/charcoal.
Medium heat (magnet): petrol, mauve, rosewood.
High heat (stage/date): cool coral, berry, oxblood.
Rule: one high-heat element at a time unless you want chaos.

Textures That Make Your Palette Look Rich SD x SS

Soft Summer needs diffusion. Soft Dramatic needs luxury. Combine with texture.

  • Velvet, brushed knits, cashmere, suede
  • Satin (matte finish), silk crepe, ponte
  • Leather: charcoal/oxblood/petrol only

“Do Not Buy” Color Traps hard no

These will make you look tired, brassy, or weirdly flat — even if you like them in theory.

  • Peach, apricot, orange-coral, terracotta
  • Camel, warm tan, honey beige
  • Gold-yellow, mustard, banana
  • Neon anything (especially teal/pink)
Makeup Translation (Soft Summer Deep Edition)

Quick conversions so your face matches your palette’s physics.

Lips: cool rosy coral, raspberry smoke, rosewood, soft oxblood (stains > opaque).
Blush: dusty rose / mauve-rose / cool rose-coral (avoid peach).
Eyes: indigo, charcoal, petrol; shimmer = pearl/soft pewter, not gold.
Bronzer: if needed, keep it neutral-rose; avoid orange bronzers.

Metals, Stones, & Hardware (Soft Dramatic × Soft Summer)

Your metals should look like they have a story. Not shiny-new. Not yellow. Not chrome.

Best metals: brushed silver, pewter, white gold, platinum, oxidized silver, gunmetal.

Soft yes: rose gold (muted, not pinky), antique gold (very pale + brushed) only if it reads champagne—not yellow.

Avoid: bright yellow gold, shiny warm gold, high-chrome mirror finishes.

Best stones: moonstone, labradorite, smoky quartz, grey pearl, amethyst (smoky), sapphire (dusty), aquamarine (greyed), emerald (deep, not neon), rose quartz (cool), garnet (cool), opal (cool fire).

Hardware rule: if the bag hardware looks like “banana gold,” it will fight you. Choose silver/pewter/gunmetal.

Makeup Kit: The “Soft Summer Deep” Face (you, translated)

This is the makeup map that matches your palette physics: cool-neutral skin, rose-based warmth, depth tolerance, diffusion.

LIPS (power families):

  • Cool Rosy Coral: “alive” without peach. (Think: rosy watermelon.)
  • Rosewood Smoke: everyday, sexy-neutral.
  • Raspberry Smoke: juicy stain energy (no neon).
  • Soft Oxblood (Cool): dramatic, controlled, adult.
  • Deep Mauve Smoke: your intellectual nude.

BLUSH: dusty rose, mauve-rose, cool rose-coral. Apply high (you already do) for SD lift. Avoid peach/apricot.

EYES: charcoal, indigo, petrol, mauve-plum. Shimmer: pearl/soft pewter. Avoid warm gold shimmer near the inner lid.

LINER: indigo > black; charcoal > black; petrol as a secret weapon.

HIGHLIGHT: moonlight pearl / soft champagne (cool). Avoid yellow highlights.

Nails + “Small Spells” (high ROI details)

If you want maximum RFG impact with minimal effort, do it in nails, earrings, and a single lip.

Nail bests: cool rosy coral, mauve-rose, berry stain, smoked plum, ink navy, pewter shimmer.

Worst nails: orange-red, coral-peach, warm nude, yellow beige, shiny gold glitter.

Earring cheat: gunmetal hoops + cool coral lip = instant “unfileable.”

Hair Color Guardrails (so you don’t get betrayed by brass)

Because your coloring is cool-neutral, hair warmth moves your whole palette. Keep it smoky.

Best direction: ash brunette, mushroom brown, cool espresso, cool brond, smoky balayage.

If you go lighter: beige-ash / champagne-ash only. (No honey, no caramel ribbons.)

Red flags: copper, auburn, golden highlights, anything that looks “sun-kissed” in an orange way.

Shopping Translator (what brands call your colors)

Because stores love lying.

  • Your “navy” = ink, midnight, deep marine, storm navy.
  • Your “coral” = rosy coral, watermelon, guava, pink grapefruit, rose-coral.
  • Your “neutral” = slate, pewter, mushroom, smoke, stone, dove.
  • Your “yellow” = oat, chamomile, straw, parchment, vanilla smoke.
  • Your “green” = petrol, juniper, lagoon, deep teal, seaweed.
